Cards

Container components for marketplace listings, profiles, content, and structured information across the WhaleLord platform.

Overview

Cards are versatile container components that group related information and actions. They provide a consistent way to display products, suppliers, orders, and content across desktop, tablet, and mobile devices.

Design Philosophy

WhaleLord cards prioritize clarity and efficiency for B2B workflows. Subtle borders and minimal shadows keep focus on content while maintaining clear visual hierarchy. Hover states provide feedback without distraction.

Design Principles

Consistent Visual Hierarchy

All cards use white backgrounds on Shore-base surface with 1px Pearl-200 borders. Title is always the largest text element, followed by supporting information in descending importance.

Subtle Hover Feedback

Border darkens from Pearl-200 to Pearl-400, titles change to Waves-500, and blog cards show background tint. No lift or shadow effects—keeping interactions professional and refined.

Mobile-First Grid Strategy

Product cards maintain 2-column grid even on mobile to maximize browsing efficiency. Other card types collapse to single column for readability on small screens.

Component Reuse

Cards integrate existing design system components—certification badges, status indicators, and buttons—ensuring consistency across the platform.

Content-First Design

Cards use DM Sans (var(--font-heading)) for all UI text with Inter (var(--font-body)) reserved for numerical data like quantities and weights. No colored text except semantic states.

Usage Guidelines

When to Use Cards

  • Displaying collections of similar items (products, suppliers, blog posts)
  • Grouping related information that needs clear visual separation
  • Creating scannable grids for browsing and comparison
  • Presenting actionable items with clear CTAs

When NOT to Use Cards

  • Single pieces of information that don't need visual separation
  • Detailed content better suited for full page layouts
  • Data tables where comparison of specific values is critical
  • Lists where simple text rows would be clearer

Implementation

Cards use CSS Grid with automatic responsive behavior. Import cards.css and use the appropriate grid class:

CSS Grid Classes 
<!-- Product Grid (4 → 3 → 2 columns) -->
<div class="card-grid-products">
  <!-- Product cards here -->
</div>

<!-- Generic Grid (3 → 2 → 1 columns) -->
<!-- Use for Supplier & Checkout Cards -->
<div class="card-grid">
  <!-- Cards here -->
</div>

<!-- Blog Grid (3 → 2 → 1 columns, wider gaps) -->
<div class="card-grid-blog">
  <!-- Blog cards here -->
</div>
